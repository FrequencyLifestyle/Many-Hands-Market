The second gathering

We didn't just dream it — we did it. And now we do it again.

In June we opened the barn doors for the very first time. Growers, makers and healers spilled out into the fields; there was real food and live music, a fire to gather around, and more warmth than any of us expected. The barn was full. So were our hearts.

Many Hands Market was Karina's small, beautiful invitation — built on a feeling she has carried most of her life: that we are all powerful creators, each of us with skills worth sharing, and that something quietly important happens when we make things with our hands and bring them to one another. June proved it true.

So we are doing it again, and again after that. On 2 August we return as the first harvest comes in — high summer, the fields turning gold, the first loaves on the table. For the people, by the people. From field to hearth, by many hands.

— Karina Clappison, founder & field-keeper

— What you'll find on the day

A few hours of good things,
gathered in for harvest.

Growers, makers & healers

The heart of it all — local stalls brimming with things grown, made and tended by hand. Many faces you met in June, and new ones joining the circle.

Demonstrations & workshops

Pull up a stool and learn something. Watch a craft take shape, try it yourself, and carry a little of the know-how home with you.

Slow food & street food

Real, honest food at the height of the season — slow plates and street-food traders, made to be eaten unhurried in good company.

Live acoustic music

Music drifting through the afternoon as the light softens over the fields — gentle, live, and easy to lose an hour to.

A fire to gather around

There is always a fire at the centre of things — a place to sit, to warm your hands as the afternoon turns to evening, and to fall into conversation with someone new.

A kids' corner & ice cream van

A corner kept just for the little ones, and an ice cream van for the warm afternoon. Things to taste, to take home, and to learn — for every age.
